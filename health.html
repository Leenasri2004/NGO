Health Services

What Happens At Our Health Camps

Each camp is designed so that people receive not only treatment, but also information and follow-up support that keeps them healthier in the long term. [web:319][web:330]

General Check-up

Basic OPD consultation, BP, sugar checks and prescriptions by qualified doctors.

Women’s Health Desk

Focus on maternal health, anaemia, menstrual health and counselling in a private, safe space.

Mind & Wellbeing Corner

Simple mental health screening, listening support and referrals where needed.

Tele-Consult Follow-up

For select cases, our team schedules tele-consults with partner specialists after the camp.

A Day On The Field

How A Typical Health Camp Runs

This simple flow keeps the camp organised and ensures that those who need the most help are not missed.

Step 01
Community Mobilisation

Volunteers and local leaders inform families, give tokens and prioritise elderly, children and pregnant women.

Step 02
Screening & Vitals

At the first desk, basic details, vitals and quick screening questions are recorded for each person.

Step 03
Doctor Consultation

Doctors examine patients, prescribe medicines, and mark those who need referral or tele-consult.

Step 04
Medicines & Follow-up

The pharmacy desk gives basic medicines while field staff plan home visits or calls for follow-up.
