WHY WOMEN EMPOWERMENT?

When Women Rise, Communities Move Forward

Gender equality is not only a human right – it is a powerful driver of health, education and economic growth for entire communities, as highlighted in global goals like the SDGs. [web:353][web:354]

Nathi Foundation walks with women and adolescent girls so that they can access healthcare, complete their education, build livelihoods and lead change in their own streets and villages.

What We Do

Building Confidence, Health And Livelihoods

Like Smile Foundation’s Swabhiman, Nathi Foundation’s women initiative focuses on health, nutrition and livelihood so that women can stand on their own feet.

Through group meetings, home visits and training, women become aware of their rights, learn to seek healthcare early, manage finances and start small enterprises.

We also involve men and boys as supporters, so that change is not just individual but rooted in families and neighbourhoods – similar to the “4 S model” used by Swabhiman.

Health & Rights Circles

Regular sessions on reproductive and child health, safe motherhood and gender‑based violence.

Skill & Livelihood Labs

Tailoring, crafts and micro‑enterprise support so women can earn and save .

Digital & Financial Literacy

Training on mobile banking, UPI, savings and government schemes for women and older girls.

Change Agents Network

Selected women and adolescents are mentored as “change agents” who guide others community.

Our Interventions

From Girls To Grandmothers – A Lifecycle Approach

Reflecting the lifecycle idea used in Swabhiman, Nathi Foundation walks with women across different stages of life – from adolescence to motherhood and beyond.

Reproductive & Child Health

Awareness sessions, health camps and referrals so women can access antenatal care, safe delivery and child immunisation at the right time.

Nutrition Enhancement

Cooking demos, low‑cost recipes and nutrition kits that help women fight anaemia and undernutrition .

Entrepreneurship Development

Business skills, market linkages and mentoring to help women start and grow micro‑enterprises at home.

Digital Financial Literacy

Hands‑on practice with phones, apps and digital payments so women feel confident using technology .

Sustainability In Communities

Women’s groups lead local initiatives in cleanliness, kitchen gardens,child protection committees.

Govt. Convergence & Systems

Linking women to schemes, SHGs and local bodies so that change is supported by formal systems .

Stories Of Change

From Silence To Strong Voice

Behind every statistic is a woman who chose to take one more step towards confidence, safety and dignity.

Health & Confidence
“I Can Talk To The Doctor Myself”

After months of health sessions and support from her group, a young mother from a nearby village went alone to the health centre for a check‑up – something she had never done before.

Today she helps neighbours understand their reports and encourages them .

Livelihood
From Sewing For Family To Running A Unit

Training and mentoring helped one woman turn her tailoring skill into a small stitching unit, employing two other women from her lane.

Her income now supports school fees and savings, and she dreams of training more girls in her area.
