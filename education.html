Why Education

Education Changes The Story For A Whole Family

When a child in Tiruppathur or a nearby village finishes school, it does not only change their mark sheet – it slowly changes the choices their entire family can make.

  • Reduces early drop‑out and child labour
  • Delays early marriage for girls
  • Opens doors to better work opportunities
  • Builds confidence, problem‑solving and voice

1 in 5

children in many low‑income neighbourhoods are at risk of dropping around middle school.

Girls First

We intentionally reach out to girls who are often the first to be pulled out of school.

Nathi Foundation follows government school curriculum, local context and simple teaching tools so children are not left behind.
What We Do

Education Support For Different Stages

Like Smile Foundation’s multi‑layered education initiatives, Nathi Foundation supports children at different ages – from early learning to bridge courses for those who dropped out. [web:281][web:283]

Early school Learning (3–6 yrs)

Play‑based learning spaces that prepare younger children to enter and adjust to formal school.

Non‑Formal Learning (6–14 yrs)

Basic literacy and numeracy for children who have never been to school or attend irregularly.

Remedial Support (6–14 yrs)

After‑school sessions that help school‑going children catch up in reading, writing and maths.

Bridge extra Courses (14–18 yrs)

Focused support for teenagers who dropped out, helping them return to school or skill training.

Our Approach

Four Pillars Of Our Education Work

Inspired by proven education models, including Mission Education by Smile Foundation, we focus on both learning inside the classroom and the support system around each child. [web:281][web:286]

Partner In Education
01
Improving Learning

Curriculum‑linked coaching, STEM activities, digital aids and regular assessments to strengthen reading, writing and maths.

02
Safe Learning Spaces

Child‑friendly centres, basic infrastructure, and a warm environment where children feel seen and safe.

03
Health & Nutrition

Health awareness, check‑ups through partners, and simple nutrition support so children can focus in class.

04
Family & Community

Regular parent meetings, home visits and community events that keep families involved in each child’s journey.

Stories

Faces Behind The Numbers

Like Smile Foundation, Nathi believes each child’s story is as important as the overall data. Here are a few quiet milestones from our classrooms. [web:283][web:284]

Girl Child
“I Did Not Drop Out”

A middle‑school girl from a nearby village was about to leave school to help at home. Regular tutoring and home visits helped her family see another option.

Today she is preparing for her board exams and tutors younger children in her lane.

Bridge Course
Back To Classroom After Two Years

A teenage boy who had been working in a shop joined Nathi’s bridge classes. Step by step, he caught up on missed basics.

With support from his family and the centre, he enrolled again in formal school.

Volunteer
From Student To Mentor

One of our first students now returns twice a week as a volunteer, helping children with homework and drawing.

She says, “Someone once sat with me after school. Now I can be that person for them.”
