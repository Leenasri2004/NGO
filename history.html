The Story Behind Nathi

Not a list of dates, but a path of small, steady steps in Nagercoil.

OUR JOURNEY

From One Room To Many Homes

Nathi Foundation did not begin with a big launch. It began when a few people stopped, listened and stayed with children who were slipping out of school.

This page maps how that quiet start slowly spread into lanes, classrooms, health camps and women’s groups across our neighbourhoods.

Hover over each era to see what changed on the ground.
Children Health Women
2015–2017 01

Lantern Evenings

After‑school corners lit by borrowed bulbs and borrowed time.

In the first season of Nathi, volunteers sat with a handful of children in a rented room, helping them read textbooks that had become too heavy to open alone.

Parents were often daily wage earners. A child passing a class felt as big as a festival. That is where Nathi learnt its first lesson: change begins in very small rooms.

2018–2019 02

Putting Our Name On Paper

From “a group that helps” to a registered foundation.

As more families began to ask for support, Nathi registered as a trust and committed to show up regularly, not just during exam season or festivals.

Timetables were drawn, attendance was tracked and simple systems were put in place. Local teachers, college students and neighbours stepped in to own the work.

Trust registration Regular centres Parent meetings
2020–2022 03

When Doors Closed

Lockdowns pushed us out of classrooms and deeper into homes.

During the pandemic, crowded tuition rooms were no longer safe. Nathi’s team began carrying learning sheets, grocery kits and phone data recharges to the doorsteps of families.

Simple phone calls became counselling sessions. Health referrals, emergency support and small cash transfers helped families breathe through the uncertainty.

Relief kits Phone learning Emergency help
2023–Present 04

Roots And Routes

Deeper relationships with children, stronger paths for women.

Today, Nathi blends learning support, health awareness and women’s livelihood training. Children stay in school longer, and mothers find new ways to add to family income.

The team now includes former students, youth leaders and partners who once received support. The history of Nathi is slowly being written by the same community it serves.

PEOPLE BEHIND THE TURNING POINTS

Faces You May Recognise

Nathi’s history is stitched together by people who were already part of the neighbourhood – teachers, auto drivers, home‑makers, students and small business owners.

Some gave time, some gave ideas, some shared space in their own homes. Many of them still drop in quietly to ask one question: “What do the children need next?”
